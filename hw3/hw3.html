EPSG: 3857 Projection

This rectangular projection maintains shape and direction, but distorts the area of the North and South.

EPSG: 53018 Projection

This projection is more rounded, and strikes a balance between shape distortion and size distortion.

EPSG: 54034 Projection

This was made by wrapping a cylinder around the globe. The North and South of the globe are very stretched out, while the equator is the closest to being truly represented..

EPSG: 54027 Projection

This map was made by wrapping a cone around a portion of the globe, leaving a cutout because the map is shaped like a circle. The parts of the map equivalent to the bottom of the cone are larger and more stretched than at the top of the cone.

EPSG: 102016

This was made by projecting part of the globe around a chosen point, here the north pole, to create a circular map. Farther away from the north pole, the map is both stretched and larger.

EPSG: 54008

This projection minimizes distortion at the equator, but the North and South of the map are strangely stretched and too large.

EPSG: 54030

This map compromises between shape and area distortion, and gets more distorted away from both the equator and the prime meridian.
